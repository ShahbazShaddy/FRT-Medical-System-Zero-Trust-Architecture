Functional Reach Test (FRT) - Help & FAQ

1. What is the Functional Reach Test (FRT)?

The Functional Reach Test (FRT) is a quick and simple clinical tool designed to assess dynamic balance and fall risk in elderly and frail adults. It measures how far a person can reach forward without moving their feet, helping predict the likelihood of falls.

2. Who should perform the FRT?

The FRT is primarily intended for:

  • Older adults in the community or clinical settings.
  • Frail adults who are at risk of falling.

3. How is the Functional Reach Test performed?

  1. Stand close to a wall without touching it.
  2. Raise the arm closest to the wall at shoulder height (90° flexion) with a closed fist.
  3. Measure the starting position of the 3rd metacarpal head (knuckle).
  4. Reach as far forward as possible without moving your feet or losing balance.
  5. Measure the end position of the 3rd metacarpal head.

The difference between start and end points is the reach distance. Three trials are done, and the average of the last two is used for scoring.

4. How are FRT scores interpreted?

Reach Distance Fall Risk
≥ 25 cm (10 inches) Low risk of falling
15–25 cm (6–10 inches) 2x greater fall risk
≤ 15 cm (6 inches) 4x greater fall risk
Unable/unwilling to reach 8x greater fall risk
